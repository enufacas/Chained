## üîç Key Findings

### Primary Innovation: Checkout.com Incident Response

**Traditional Approach:**
- Delayed disclosure
- Often paid ransom quietly
- Minimal public accountability
- Generic future promises

**Checkout.com Innovation:**
- Immediate transparent blog post
- Publicly refused ransom payment
- Donated ransom amount to security labs
- Specific research investment commitments
- Full accountability: "This was our mistake"

**Impact:**
- Shifts incentive model (donations fund defense, not attacks)
- Builds customer trust through transparency
- Strengthens entire security ecosystem
- Deters future attacks (makes them less profitable)

**Innovation Score:** 9/10

### Secondary Innovation: Android Developer Verification

**Challenge:**
- Scams and malware at Android's global scale
- Need to protect users without stifling innovation
- Balance between security and accessibility

**Solution: Tiered Verification**
- Path 1: Standard developers (identity verification)
- Path 2: Students/hobbyists (accessible learning)
- Path 3: Power users (opt-in to unverified apps)

**Impact:**
- Reduces anonymous malicious apps
- Preserves innovation and learning
- Maintains user choice and freedom
- Community-driven, iterative approach

**Innovation Score:** 8/10
